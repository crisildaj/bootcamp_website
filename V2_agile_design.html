Agile DSDM

    Agile DSDM is an iterative and incremental approach to managing projects. It emphasizes collaboration, flexibility, and delivering value to customers.

    Principles of DSDM

  • Focus on business need, Deliver on time, Collaborate, Never Compromise Quality, Build incrementally, Develop Iterativley, Communicate Continuously & Demonstrate control

    • DSDM Roles

  • Key roles for our project were Project Manager, Business Analyst, Technical Co-ordinator, Developers, Scrum Master & Product Owner. We combined DSDM and Scrum.

    • Processes

  • Pre-project, Feasibility, Foundations, Evolution Development, Deployment & Post Deployment

    • Practices

  • MoSCoW prioritization, Modelling, Prototyping,Workshops

    • How we applied Agile DSDM

    Pre Project we created a TOR-Terms of Reference and defined the business objectives, project objectives, what is in and out of scope. We combined Feasiability and Foundations, allocated roles and generated numerous project documents, business case, RACI matrix PAQ, SAD, DAD ,MAD and a PID. We planned to horizons and created a project timeline. We also used modelling techniques such as wire frames.

Scrum

  • 'Scrum is a lightweight framework that helps people, teams and organisations generate value through adaptive solutions for complex problems.

    • Scrum Roles

  • Scrum's three roles are; Product Owner, Scrum Master and developer.

    • Scrum Events

  • Scrum has five events, The Sprint, Sprint Planning, Daily Standup, Sprint Review and Sprint Retrospective

    • Scrum Artefacts

  • Scrum has three artefacts, Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog and The Increment.

    • How we applied Scrum

  • Initally we allocatd roles, one person assumed the role of product owner taking ownership of the product backlog, the remaining four members of the team rotated the scrum master role. All members of the team assumed the role of developer. We used four one week sprints, daily standups took place Monday - Friday via online video conferencing with cameras on. Sprint planning and estimation was based on hours, each developer showcased their work during sprint reviews and amended any work based on feedback. Sprint retrospectives highlighted process improvements which we put into the sprint backlog to apply during the next sprint. We developed Iteratively and delivered incrementally.

Design Thinking Process

  • Design thinking is a creative and user-centered approach to problem-solving that is often used in the context of web design and product development. It emphasizes understanding the needs and preferences of the end-users to create a more appealing, engaging and functional product. The design thinking process is made up of the problem space and the solution space and can be broken down into the following phases:

  • Empathize - understand the needs and perspectives of users and stakeholders.

  • Define - clearly understand the problem or challenges.

  • Ideate - generate a wide range of creative and innovative ideas.

  • Prototype - build low-fidelity representations of your ideas.

  • Test - gather feedback from stakeholders and users.

  • Implement - implement the best solution.

  • Iterate - continuoulsy refine the solution improving every iteration.

    • How we applied Design Thinking

  • By following the process we were able to explore both the problem space and the solution space identifying user pain points and exploiting opportunities. To aid us in the design process we used tools such as user persona cards, user journey mapping, double diamond and scamper models. One of the key pain points identified was 'fragmented learning experience'.
